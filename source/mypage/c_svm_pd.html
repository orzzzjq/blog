Given two point sets P={𝒖1,…,𝒖n1} and Q={𝒗1,…,𝒗n2} in 𝑹d, the reduced polytope distance problem (RPD) is to find two density distributions 𝝁,𝝀 over the input points, such that ∥𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀∥ is minimized, here 𝟏T⁢𝝁=𝟏T⁢𝝀=1 and 0≤𝝁,𝝀≤D for some constant D≤1. The idea of having the upperbound D is to prevent excessive influence of an outlier point. The problem can be formatted as the following optimization problem:

min𝝁,𝝀  12⁢∥𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀∥2 (RPD)
s.t.  𝟏T⁢𝝁=1 𝟏T⁢𝝀=1
0≤𝝁,𝝀≤D

The RPD problem is equivalent to C-SVM, but before we prove the equivalence of RPD and C-SVM, we introduce an intermittent problem, the soft-margin maximization problem (S-Margin), which can be written as the following problem:

min𝒘,𝝃,𝜼,α,β  12⁢∥𝒘∥2-(α-β)+D⁢(𝟏T⁢𝝃+𝟏T⁢𝜼) (S-Margin)
s.t.  𝑷T⁢𝒘≥α⁢𝟏-𝝃,𝝃≥0
𝑸T⁢𝒘≤β⁢𝟏+𝜼,𝜼≥0
Theorem 1.

(RPD) and (S-Margin) are dual of each other.

Proof.

Consider the Lagrangian of (S-Margin):

L⁢(𝒘,𝝃,𝜼,α,β,𝝁,𝝀,𝒓,𝒔) =12⁢∥𝒘∥2-(α-β)+D⁢(𝟏T⁢𝝃+𝟏T⁢𝜼)
-𝝁T⁢(𝑷T⁢𝒘-α⁢𝟏+𝝃)-𝒓T⁢𝝃
+𝝀T⁢(𝑸T⁢𝒘-β⁢𝟏-𝜼)-𝒔T⁢𝜼

The KKT condition for this problem is:

Stationary: ∂⁡L/∂⁡𝒘=𝒘-𝑷⁢𝝁+𝑸⁢𝝀=0
∂⁡L/∂⁡𝝃=D⁢𝟏-𝝁-𝒓=0
∂⁡L/∂⁡𝝀=D⁢𝟏-𝝀-𝒔=0
∂⁡L/∂⁡α=𝟏T⁢𝝁-1=0
∂⁡L/∂⁡β=𝟏T⁢𝝀-1=0
Complementary: 𝝁T⁢(𝑷T⁢𝒘-α⁢𝟏+𝝃)=0,𝒓T⁢𝝃=0
𝝀T⁢(𝑸T⁢𝒘-β⁢𝟏-𝜼)=0,𝒔T⁢𝜼=0
Primal feasibility: 𝑷T⁢𝒘≥α⁢𝟏-𝝃,𝝃≥0
𝑸T⁢𝒘≤β⁢𝟏+𝜼,𝜼≥0
Dual feasibility: 𝝁,𝝀,𝒓,𝒔≥0

Take 𝒘=𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀,𝒓=D⁢𝟏-𝝁,𝒔=D⁢𝟏-𝝀, the dual of (S-Margin) becomes:

max𝝁,𝝀  L⁢(𝝃,𝜼,𝝁,𝝀)
s.t.  𝟏T⁢𝝁=𝟏T⁢𝝀=1
0≤𝝁,𝝀≤D

Furthermore,

L⁢(𝝃,𝜼,𝝁,𝝀)= 12⁢∥𝒘∥2-(α-β)+D⁢(𝟏T⁢𝝃+𝟏T⁢𝜼)
-𝝁T⁢(𝑷T⁢𝒘-α⁢𝟏+𝝃)-𝒓T⁢𝝃
+𝝀T⁢(𝑸T⁢𝒘-β⁢𝟏-𝜼)-𝒔T⁢𝜼
= 12⁢∥𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀∥2-(α-β)+D⁢(𝟏T⁢𝝃+𝟏T⁢𝜼)
-𝝁T⁢𝑷T⁢(𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀)+α⁢𝟏T⁢𝝁-𝝃T⁢𝝁-(D⁢𝟏-𝝁)T⁢𝝃
+𝝀T⁢𝑸T⁢(𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀)-β⁢𝟏T⁢𝝀-𝜼T⁢𝝀-(D⁢𝟏-𝝀)T⁢𝜼
= 12⁢∥𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀∥2-(α-β)+D⁢𝟏T⁢𝝃+D⁢𝟏T⁢𝜼
-𝝁T⁢𝑷T⁢(𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀)+α-𝝃T⁢𝝁-D⁢𝟏T⁢𝝃+𝝁T⁢𝝃
+𝝀T⁢𝑸T⁢(𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀)-β-𝜼T⁢𝝀-D⁢𝟏T⁢𝜼+𝝀T⁢𝜼
= 12⁢∥𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀∥2-(𝝁T⁢𝑷T-𝝀T⁢𝑸T)⁢(𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀)
= -12⁢∥𝑷⁢𝝁-𝑸⁢𝝀∥2,

which is equivalent to (RPD) and completes the proof. ∎

Now we turn to the C-SVM problem. The C-SVM problem can be formatted as

min𝒘,𝝃,𝜼,γ  12⁢∥𝒘∥2+C⁢(𝟏T⁢𝝃+𝟏T⁢𝜼) (C-SVM)
s.t.  𝑷T⁢𝒘≥(γ+1)⁢𝟏-𝝃
𝑸T⁢𝒘≤(γ-1)⁢𝟏+𝜼
𝝃≥0 𝜼≥0

Here C>0 is a parameter that is chosen by the user to control the slackness. Intuitively, this problem is equivalent to (S-Margin) by fixing (α-β)=2 and set α=γ+1,β=γ-1, since maximizing the soft-margin is the same as fixing the offset and minimizing the norm of the normal vector 𝒘. The formal proof is given in Theorem 2.

Theorem 2.

With an appropriate choice of the parameters C and D, the problems (S-Margin) and (C-SVM) are equivalent if the optimal objective is strictly greater than 0.

Proof.

Recall that (𝒘¯,𝝃¯,𝜼¯,α¯,β¯,𝝁¯,𝝀¯) is a KKT point of (S-Margin) if it satisfies:

Stationary: 𝒘¯-𝑷⁢𝝁¯+𝑸⁢𝝀¯=0 (1)
D⁢𝟏-𝝁¯≥0
D⁢𝟏-𝝀¯≥0
𝟏T⁢𝝁¯-1=0
𝟏T⁢𝝀¯-1=0
Complementary: 𝝁¯T⁢(𝑷T⁢𝒘¯-α⁢𝟏+𝝃¯)=0,(D⁢𝟏-𝝁¯)T⁢𝝃¯=0
𝝀¯T⁢(𝑸T⁢𝒘¯-β⁢𝟏-𝜼¯)=0,(D⁢𝟏-𝝀¯)T⁢𝜼¯=0
Primal feasibility: 𝑷T⁢𝒘¯≥α⁢𝟏-𝝃¯,𝝃¯≥0
𝑸T⁢𝒘¯≤β⁢𝟏+𝜼¯,𝜼¯≥0
Dual feasibility: 𝝁¯,𝝀¯≥0

Each KKT point (𝒘^,𝝃^,𝜼^,γ^,𝝁^,𝝀^) of (C-SVM) satisfies:

Stationary: 𝒘^-𝑷⁢𝝁^+𝑸⁢𝝀^=0 (2)
C⁢𝟏-𝝁^≥0
C⁢𝟏-𝝀^≥0
𝟏T⁢𝝁^-𝟏T⁢𝝀^=0
Complementary: 𝝁^T⁢(𝑷T⁢𝒘^-(γ^+1)⁢𝟏+𝝃^)=0,(C⁢𝟏-𝝁^)T⁢𝝃^=0
𝝀^T⁢(𝑸T⁢𝒘^-(γ^-1)⁢𝟏-𝜼^)=0,(C⁢𝟏-𝝀^)T⁢𝜼^=0
Primal feasibility: 𝑷T⁢𝒘^≤(γ^+1)⁢𝟏-𝝃^,𝝃^≥0
𝑸T⁢𝒘^≥(γ^-1)⁢𝟏+𝜼^,𝜼^≥0
Dual feasibility: 𝝁^,𝝀^≥0

Assuming α~-β~>0, set δ=2α~-β~,α~=γ^+1δ,β~=γ^-1δ,𝒘~=𝒘^δ,𝝃~=𝝃^δ,𝜼~=𝜼^δ,𝝁~=𝝁^δ,𝝀~=𝝀^δ, and 𝟏T⁢𝝁^=𝟏T⁢𝝀^=δ, and take D=Cδ, we have:

Stationary: 𝒘~-𝑷⁢𝝁~+𝑸⁢𝝀~=0 (3)
D⁢𝟏-𝝁~≥0
D⁢𝟏-𝝀~≥0
𝟏T⁢𝝁~=𝟏T⁢𝝀~=1
Complementary: 𝝁~T⁢(𝑷T⁢𝒘~-α~⁢𝟏+𝝃~)=0,(D⁢𝟏-𝝁~)T⁢𝝃~=0
𝝀~T⁢(𝑸T⁢𝒘~-β~⁢𝟏-𝜼~)=0,(D⁢𝟏-𝝀~)T⁢𝜼~=0
Primal feasibility: 𝑷T⁢𝒘~≤α~⁢𝟏-𝝃~,𝝃~≥0
𝑸T⁢𝒘~≥β~⁢𝟏+𝜼~,𝜼~≥0
Dual feasibility: 𝝁~,𝝀~≥0

which coincides with (1), and implies that (𝒘~,𝝃~,𝜼~,α~,β~,𝝁~,𝝀~) is a KKT point to (S-Margin). The assumption α~-β~>0 is from the strong duality of (S-Margin):

12⁢∥𝒘~∥2+D⁢(𝟏T⁢𝝃~+𝟏T⁢𝜼~)-(α~-β~)=-12⁢∥𝑷⁢𝝁~-𝑸⁢𝝀~∥2<0,

where we assume the objective value is strictly greater than 0, i.e. the reduced convex hulls are linear separable. ∎
